User Surveys: The Foundation of Great UX Design

By James McEwen | November 4, 2020 | Building Websites

What is a User Survey? In the world of UX Design, User Surveys are essential in the design of any website or application. When building a new app or website there is always a bias towards the design from either the stakeholder or designers and this is not good. Designing any website or app should always begin with placing the end users needs first.

Have you ever been on an app or website and had no idea where to go on it or hit a point of frustration with the functionality of it? Well this is because the developer more than likely didn't conduct user surveys.

Why User Surveys Matter

When developers skip user surveys, they also tend to skip usability testing and prototyping. This leads to products that frustrate users and fail to meet their actual needs.

User surveys are simple surveys that a UX researcher would conduct with random people that allows them to collect valuable data that will give the design team direction on the design and flow of a great website or app. Or whether there is even a need to build such an app or website.

The Competitive Analysis Connection

There are a lot of people out there that think they might have a Great idea for an app and then jump in trying to develop it and then realize no one is using the app or that there are already apps just like it out there.

If they would have done a competitive analysis they would have found out that there is already an app for their great idea, and then conducting a user survey they would have also found out that perhaps no one else thought it was a great idea depending on the survey answers.

Key Takeaways

Before starting any web or app development project:

  • Conduct user surveys to understand your target audience
  • Perform competitive analysis to see what already exists
  • Validate your idea before investing in development
  • Always put the end user's needs first
